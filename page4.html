Statistics

The figures below are drawn from leading health and research organisations around the world. They paint a clear picture of how screen time is affecting the health and wellbeing of young people globally.

7-9 hrs

Average daily screen time for teenagers, according to Magnet Aba Therapy

70%

Of young people say social media makes them feel anxious, according to The Guardian

2x

Teenagers who use social media for more than 3 hours a day are 2 times more likely to experience depression, according to Office of the Surgeon General (OSG)

60%

Of teenagers report using their phone within an hour of going to sleep, according to Hale et al., 2018

33%

Increase in depression and anxiety among young people since smartphones became widely used, according to Twenge et al., 2018

50%

Children experience digital eye strain (DES) from excessive screen use, according to Mohan et al., 2021
