Tips & Advice

Managing your screen time doesn't have to mean giving up technology altogether. Small, simple changes to your daily habits can make a significant difference to both your mental and physical health. Here are 15 practical tips to help you build a healthier relationship with your screens.

1. Follow the 20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.

2. Set a Daily Screen Time Limit

Use your phone's built-in screen time settings to set a daily limit and stick to it.

3. Keep Phones out of the Bedroom

Avoid using your phone at least an hour before bed to improve your sleep quality.

4. Turn Off Non-Essential Notifications

Constant notifications keep your brain on high alert. Turn off anything that isn't urgent.

5. Take Regular Breaks

Stand up and move around for a few minutes every hour to reduce the effects of sitting for long periods.

6. Use Night Mode in the Evening

Enable blue light filters or night mode on your device after sunset to help your body wind down naturally.

7. Have Phone-Free Mealtimes

Put your phone away during meals. It helps you be more present and gives your eyes a proper rest.

8. Replace Scrolling With Movement

Swap 30 minutes of social media scrolling for a walk, stretch or any physical activity you enjoy.

9. Charge Your Phone Outside Your Bedroom

Keeping your phone out of reach overnight removes the temptation to check it first thing in the morning.

10. Set App Time Limits on Social Media

Most phones let you set specific time limits per app. Use this to cap how long you spend on social media each day.

11. Sit With Good Posture

Keep your screen at eye level and sit with your back straight to prevent neck and back pain over time.

12. Avoid Screens First Thing in the Morning

Try not to check your phone within the first 30 minutes of waking up. Starting the day without screens can improve your mood and focus.

13. Use Do Not Disturb Mode

Schedule do not disturb during study time, meals and sleep so you can focus without constant interruptions.

14. Be Mindful of What You Watch

Not all screen time is equal. Choose content that is positive or educational rather than mindlessly scrolling through feeds.

15. Talk to Someone if You're Struggling

If you feel like your screen use is affecting your mental health, don't hesitate to speak to a trusted adult, friend or visit your GP.
