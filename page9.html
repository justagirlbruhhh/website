Resources & Support

If you would like to learn more about screen time and its effects, or if you are struggling with your mental health and need support, the following organisations and websites provide reliable information, advice and help.

Mental Health Support

Mind

Mind is one of the leading mental health charities in England and Wales. Their website provides information on a wide range of mental health conditions including anxiety and depression, which have been linked to excessive screen time.

Visit Mind

YoungMinds

YoungMinds is the UK's leading charity fighting for children and young people's mental health. They have specific resources on social media, screen time and how to get help if you are struggling.

Visit YoungMinds

Samaritans

Samaritans provide confidential emotional support 24 hours a day for anyone who is struggling. You can call them free on 116 123 at any time of day or night.

Visit Samaritans

Childline

Childline offers free and confidential advice for young people up to the age of 19. They cover topics including online safety, mental health and screen time addiction.

Visit Childline

Health & Wellbeing

NHS — Screen Time Advice

The NHS provides clear and trusted guidance on screen time recommendations for children and young people, including advice on sleep, physical activity and mental health.

Visit NHS

World Health Organisation

The WHO has published global guidelines on physical activity and sedentary behaviour, including screen time recommendations for different age groups.

Visit WHO

Sleep Foundation

The Sleep Foundation provides research backed information on how screens and blue light affect sleep quality, and offers practical advice on improving your sleep habits.

Visit Sleep Foundation

Online Safety & Digital Wellbeing

Internet Matters

Internet Matters is a non profit organisation that helps families stay safe online. They have specific guides on managing screen time for children and young people.

Visit Internet Matters

Common Sense Media

Common Sense Media provides reviews and advice on media and technology for families, including detailed guides on screen time management for different age groups.

Visit Common Sense Media

UK Safer Internet Centre

The UK Safer Internet Centre promotes the safe and responsible use of technology for young people. Their website includes resources for students, parents and teachers.

Visit UK Safer Internet Centre
